Decision-making

One of the most crucial steps in any machine learning project is to select the right technique for the job. Your choice depends entirely on the problem you're trying to solve and the type of data you have. The tables below will help you identify the best approach for your specific use case.

Use Case Comparison Table

Problem Type
ML Technique
Common Examples
Predicting a continuous value
Regression (Supervised Learning)
Predicting house prices, forecasting stock values, estimating sales revenue, predicting a person's life expectancy, forecasting weather temperatures.
Assigning data to a category
Classification (Supervised Learning)
Detecting spam emails, identifying a disease from a medical scan, determining if a customer will churn, classifying images of animals, sentiment analysis of customer reviews.
Finding hidden groups in data
Clustering (Unsupervised Learning)
Segmenting customers for marketing, grouping similar news articles, organizing genes with similar functions, grouping documents by topic, identifying different species of flowers.
Identifying unusual or rare data points
Anomaly Detection (Unsupervised Learning)
Fraud detection in banking, identifying faulty equipment, spotting network intrusions, detecting bot traffic on a website, spotting a compromised account.
